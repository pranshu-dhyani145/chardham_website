The First Dham

Yamunotri — Origin of the Yamuna

Yamunotri is the westernmost shrine of the Char Dham circuit, dedicated to Goddess Yamuna — the daughter of Surya (Sun God) and twin sister of Yama, the God of Death. It is believed that bathing in the Yamuna cleanses one of all sins and grants liberation from the fear of death.

The actual source of the Yamuna river lies at the Champasar Glacier on the Kalind Parvat peak (~4,421m), about 1 km above the temple. Devotees carry water from the Surya Kund hot spring and cook rice in its boiling waters as an offering — a ritual unique to Yamunotri.

Temple & Surroundings

Key Highlights of Yamunotri

♨️

Surya Kund

The most sacred hot spring at Yamunotri with near-boiling temperatures (~94°C). Pilgrims cook rice and potatoes in muslin cloth here as prasad offerings to the goddess.

🏛️

Yamunotri Temple

Built by Maharani Guleria of Jaipur in the 19th century and later rebuilt after earthquake damage. Houses a black marble idol of Goddess Yamuna and a silver idol of Ganga.

❄️

Divya Shila

A sacred rock pillar situated near the temple that is worshipped before entering the main shrine. Devotees offer prayers here as part of the traditional ritual.

🌊

Janki Chatti

The base camp for the Yamunotri trek at 2,650m. All vehicles stop here. The 6 km paved trek begins from this village, passing scenic forests and the Yamuna river.

🛕

Kharsali Village

The winter home of the Yamunotri deity. During the winter closure, the idol is shifted to this village temple 3 km from Janki Chatti where worship continues till spring.

🌺

Saptrishi Kund

A glacial lake at ~4,500m above the temple, said to be the abode of the seven sages. Accessible only by a difficult high-altitude trek, rarely visited but deeply sacred.

Visitor Essentials

Tips for Yamunotri Yatra

🥾

The 6 km Trek

The paved trail from Janki Chatti takes 2–3 hours each way. Ponies and palanquins (dolis) are available for those who cannot walk.

🌡️

Weather & Clothing

Even in May–June, temperatures drop sharply after sunset. Carry thermal layers, waterproof jacket, gloves, and sturdy trekking shoes.

🍚

Surya Kund Prasad

Bring raw rice in a muslin cloth to cook in the hot spring. This is the unique Yamunotri tradition — the cooked rice is taken home as blessed prasad.

📋

Registration

Biometric registration is mandatory. Register online at the official Uttarakhand Char Dham Devasthanam portal before departure.

🏨

Accommodation

Basic dharamshalas and GMVN guesthouses are available at Janki Chatti and Barkot. Book well in advance during May–June peak season.

⏰

Best Time

May–June for the classic yatra. September–October for smaller crowds and clearer post-monsoon mountain views. Temple remains closed Nov–Apr.
